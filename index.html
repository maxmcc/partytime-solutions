Gallery Opening Event

The Steiner & Co. art gallery, which is definitely a real thing that exists, will host an opening party on Saturday the 16th of April 2022 at 6pm. Add the event to Google Calendar ↗ or Apple Calendar ↗.

The gallery will showcase one-of-a-kind artwork from a range of talented artists attending the event (including you). Guests will participate in a silent auction and will bid for the opportunity to bring home one of the items on display, with all auction proceeds donated to the San Francisco–Marin Food Bank.

Make and Bring Your Own Artwork

We ask that you please do not bring gifts to the party. In lieu of gifts, your artistic talents and financial patronage will support an important charitable cause.

Admittance to the event for any artists donating their personal work is, naturally, free.

Those wishing to attend in a non-artistic capacity (i.e., you forget to make or bring your own art) will be asked to make a suggested donation of $25. We strongly prefer that all invitees challenge themselves to produce some piece of art and donate it to our auction, no matter their skill level.

Please only bring art that is your own work, and which you are prepared to give away. Please only bring art that you are also prepared to take home with you. Artistic collaboration with other attendees of the event is perfectly acceptable.

Types of Art Our Gallery Collects

As this is a gallery, not a museum, the items contributed to our collection must have some sort of physical embodiment. Digital artworks such as illustrations or photography should be printed out, though you may also send a copy of the digital originals to the purchaser in addition to the prints.

Performed works such as songs or interpretive dances may be acceptable, so long as some component (e.g., lyrics) can be purchased and taken home.

We only accept finished artworks, so please do not plan on making additions to your project during the event. We do not have a color printer or high quality paper at the gallery.

Attending the Event

Please arrive around 6pm with your artwork in hand. If you are not sure of the gallery’s address, please contact one of the staff members (Max or Travis).

You are welcome to invite your partners or friends to this event. They are also strongly encouraged to produce and submit an art piece for the auction. Please direct them to this website and ensure that they fill out the RSVP form.

The gallery requires patrons to be up to date ↗ on all vaccinations, including (but not limited to) full vaccination and booster doses for SARS-CoV-2, the virus that causes COVID-19.

RSVP Form

Please have each person attending the event fill out this form separately. Even if you can’t make it, please still consider making a donation ↗ to support our fundraiser. But we hope to see you there!

What to Wear

There is no specific dress code for this event, but artists and patrons alike should remember that in the world of fine art, it is always important to make a statement. Consider your own personal brand and how best to represent yourself and your work.

Everybody gets stuck in a creative rut now and again, and so it can be helpful to consult a set of prompts to find inspiration. To that end, here are some suggestions:

  • Something you otherwise have no reason to wear
  • Dirty paint smocks
  • Weird tie-dye
  • Billowing, formless shapes
  • A very uncommon hat
  • Your fifth grade art teacher
  • Your fifth grade art teacher’s worst student
  • Pick one of Brian Eno’s Oblique Strategies ↗

Program Schedule

6pm

Arrive and set up your artwork for display

Depending on your work of art, the gallery will assign you space on a shelf, table, or wall. Once you have installed your piece, the party can begin!

6–8pm

Artists’ reception featuring small bites and bubbles

Meet your fellow artists, mingle with the patrons of the gallery, and sample a wide assortment of hors-d’œuvres fresh from the Trader Joe’s freezer aisle. As is required for a fancy event, sparkling wine and cider willl be provided.

8pm

Presentation of works, art criticism, and appraisal

We will take a tour of the gallery together and view each of the pieces that are up for auction. Bidders will hear directly from the artists about their work, and will have the opportunity to ask them questions and provide thoughtful critique.

10pm

Bidding in the silent auction concludes

At last, the auction winners are revealed. Donations will be collected and the winners will return home triumphant with their new artwork.
